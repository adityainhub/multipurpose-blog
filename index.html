What Is Exposure Bracketing In Photography?

Exposure bracketing is one of the most useful techniques in photography. In simple words, it means taking multiple photos of the same subject with different exposure settings. Therefore, bracketing is about changing your exposure: one photo at the camera meter’s recommendation, one under-exposed, and one over-exposed. Then the three photos could be merged into one photo using editing software to produce a High Dynamic Range (HDR) photo. This article explains everything you need to know about bracketing, what is it, its types, and how to use it in order to take take the best possible photos.
